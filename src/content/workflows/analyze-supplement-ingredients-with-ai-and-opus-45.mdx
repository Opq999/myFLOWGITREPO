## What you'll get
You will learn how to use an AI model to analyze supplement ingredients and cross-reference them with scientific research. This workflow helps you distinguish between scientifically-backed supplements and those with less evidence, empowering informed purchasing decisions.

## Tools you need
*   **Opus 4.5** (paid): An AI model used here for analyzing and interpreting research data.
*   **Web Browser**: To access AI tools and research papers.

## Steps
1.  **Access the AI model**: Navigate to the Opus 4.5 interface. The author does not specify the exact steps for accessing Opus 4.5, but typically this involves logging into their platform.
2.  **Prepare your data**: Gather the list of ingredients from a supplement you want to analyze. You will also need access to research papers or summaries related to supplementation. The author mentions indexing research papers and ranking claims by effect type and size, but does not provide specific data sources or formats. You will need to find relevant research papers yourself.
3.  **Prompt the AI for ingredient analysis**: The author does not share their exact prompt for analyzing ingredients. A starting point could be:
    ```
    Analyze the following supplement ingredients: [List of ingredients here]. For each ingredient, identify its primary claimed benefits and the scientific evidence supporting those claims. Please also note any potential side effects or interactions mentioned in research.
    ```
    You should receive a breakdown of each ingredient, its purported uses, and a summary of supporting scientific evidence.
4.  **Prompt the AI for research paper analysis**: The author describes indexing research papers and ranking claims. You can adapt this by asking the AI to summarize and rank research.
    ```
    Review the following research paper abstract/summary: [Paste research paper text here]. Rank the claims made about supplementation based on effect size and type of evidence. Identify the strongest scientific claims.
    ```
    You should get a ranked list of claims from the research, highlighting the most scientifically supported ones.
5.  **Link supplement data to research**: The author's third step is to link supplement data with research papers. This requires comparing the AI's analysis of the supplement ingredients with its analysis of the research papers. You will need to manually compare the outputs from steps 3 and 4 to see if the supplement's ingredients are supported by the scientific evidence you've analyzed.

## Notes & variations
*   **Free Tier Alternative**: If Opus 4.5 is not accessible, you can use free AI chat tools like Claude.ai, ChatGPT (free tier), or Gemini.google.com. Be aware that free models may be more prone to 'hallucinations' (generating incorrect information), so cross-referencing their output with original research is crucial.
*   **Common Pitfall**: Do not blindly trust the AI's output. Always verify the AI's summaries and rankings against the original research papers, especially when dealing with health-related information.
*   **Tip for Better Results**: When prompting the AI, be as specific as possible about the type of information you need. For example, instead of asking for 'benefits', ask for 'clinically proven benefits' or 'anecdotal evidence'.
