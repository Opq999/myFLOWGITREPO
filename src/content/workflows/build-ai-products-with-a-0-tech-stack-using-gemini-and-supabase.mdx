## What you'll get
You will learn how to assemble a tech stack for building and deploying AI-powered products that costs little to nothing. This approach is ideal for individuals and small teams looking to experiment with AI development without significant financial investment.

## Tools you need
*   **Google's AntiGravity** (free) - An Integrated Development Environment (IDE) for writing and running code.
*   **SuperDocs** (free) - A tool for generating AI documentation.
*   **Supabase** (freemium) - A backend-as-a-service platform providing database, authentication, and more.
*   **Stack Auth** (freemium) - An authentication service for managing user sign-ups and logins.
*   **Gemini** (freemium) - A large language model (LLM) for AI capabilities.
*   **AI Studio** (freemium) - A platform for testing and developing with AI models like Gemini.
*   **Replit** (freemium) - An online IDE and platform for hosting and running applications.

## Steps
1.  **Set up your development environment** — Access Google's AntiGravity via your web browser. This will be your primary coding space.
    You should see a code editor interface, similar to other online IDEs.
2.  **Integrate AI documentation generation** — Use SuperDocs to help document your AI projects. The author does not specify exact steps for integration, but it typically involves connecting it to your codebase or using its interface to generate documentation from your code or prompts.
    You should have a way to generate documentation for your AI features.
3.  **Configure your database** — Sign up for Supabase and select the Nano plan, which is free and sufficient for basic needs. Use it to set up your project's database.
    You should have a Supabase project dashboard with a database ready to store your application's data.
4.  **Implement user authentication** — Utilize Stack Auth to handle user sign-ups and logins. The free tier supports up to 10,000 users, making it suitable for many small projects.
    You should have a system in place to manage user accounts securely.
5.  **Choose your AI model** — For testing and development, use Gemini via AI Studio. For production, consider a custom-tuned model using Unsloth AI (though the excerpt doesn't detail Unsloth's usage, it's mentioned as an option for production).
    You should have access to an AI model for your application's intelligent features.
6.  **Develop and test your application** — Use Replit as your online IDE to build and test your AI product. The author found Replit to provide the best results among several tools they tried.
    You should be able to write, run, and debug your code within the Replit environment.

## Notes & variations
*   **Free Tier Focus**: This workflow heavily relies on free tiers. Ensure you monitor usage limits for Supabase and Stack Auth to stay within the free allowances.
*   **Common Pitfall**: Over-reliance on a single tool without understanding its limitations. The author experimented with multiple tools before settling on this stack, highlighting the importance of testing.
*   **Get Better Results**: Explore AI Studio's capabilities for fine-tuning Gemini models for specific tasks, which can lead to more tailored and efficient AI responses in your application.
