## What you'll get

You'll learn a practical workflow for collaborating with AI language models (LLMs) to write a book. This approach uses LLMs as a sounding board, drafting partner, and editor, helping overcome writer's block and refine content.

## Tools you need

*   **ChatGPT** (freemium) — A versatile AI chatbot for brainstorming, drafting, and refining text.
*   **Claude** (freemium) — Another powerful AI chatbot, useful for conversational brainstorming and text generation.
*   **Gemini** (freemium) — Google's AI chatbot, suitable for similar tasks as ChatGPT and Claude.

## Steps

1.  **Capture raw ideas immediately** — Use your preferred LLM's mobile app (or website) to quickly record ideas as they come. You can use voice dictation or type them out. The goal is to get your thoughts down without worrying about perfection.
    *   **What to do:** Open your chosen LLM (e.g., ChatGPT) and start a new chat. If using voice, activate the dictation feature on your phone. Speak your ideas clearly.
    *   **What you should see:** Raw, potentially rambling text that captures your initial thoughts. This is the "spark" to be refined later.

2.  **Brainstorm and explore ideas conversationally** — Treat the AI as a partner to "talk" through your ideas. Ask it for analogies, different perspectives, or potential structures for your book.
    *   **What to do:** Paste your raw ideas or a summary into the chat. Ask open-ended questions like: "What are some analogies for [concept]?" or "Can you help me brainstorm different ways to structure a chapter on [topic]?"
    *   **What you should see:** A list of suggestions, counterarguments, or structural ideas that can help you develop your initial thoughts further.

3.  **Generate first drafts when stuck** — If you're facing writer's block, ask the AI to generate a first pass of a section. Be specific about the target audience and the desired tone.
    *   **What to do:** Provide a clear instruction, such as: `Explain [concept] simply for a beginner audience.` or `Write a draft introduction for a chapter about [topic].`
    *   **What you should see:** A block of text that serves as a starting point. Remember, this is raw material and will require significant human editing.

4.  **Write first, then have AI polish** — Alternatively, write a section yourself and then ask the AI to improve it.
    *   **What to do:** Paste your written text into the chat and ask for specific improvements, e.g., `Can you polish this paragraph for clarity and conciseness?` or `Make this section sound more engaging.`
    *   **What you should see:** An improved version of your text, with enhanced flow, grammar, or style.

5.  **Engage in iterative refinement** — This is the core loop of the process. Paste your draft text and ask for specific feedback, then integrate the AI's suggestions selectively.
    *   **What to do:** Paste a section of your draft and ask a precise question: `Is the logic in this paragraph clear?` or `Does this explanation of [term] make sense?` Review the AI's feedback and decide which suggestions to incorporate.
    *   **What you should see:** Specific feedback on your text, followed by an updated version of your draft after you've integrated the useful suggestions.

## Original source

This workflow is based on lessons learned shared by scottfalconer on Hacker News, detailing a months-long project of writing a book collaboratively with AI language models like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

## Notes & variations

*   **Free-tier viability:** All mentioned LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) have generous free tiers that are perfectly adequate for this workflow. You can switch between them to leverage their different strengths.
*   **Common pitfall:** Avoid vague prompts. Asking the AI to "write something" will yield generic results. Always provide context, specify the audience, desired tone, and the exact task.
*   **Tip for better results:** Treat the AI as a partner, not a replacement for your own thinking. Use its output as raw material, always fact-checking and editing heavily to ensure accuracy and maintain your unique voice.
