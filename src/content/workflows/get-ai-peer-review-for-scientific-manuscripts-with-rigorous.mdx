## What you'll get

A detailed feedback report on your scientific paper before you submit it anywhere, the kind of critique a journal reviewer gives, but in a day instead of months. ("Peer review" is the expert-feedback step journals use to vet research.) Rigorous runs your paper past a set of specialized AI reviewers that check the methodology (how the study was done), the scientific rigor, and the writing, then hands you specific things to fix.

## Tools you need

- **Rigorous** (free during testing): upload your paper, get back a review report. No setup.

## Steps

1. **Open Rigorous**: go to rigorous.company on your phone or computer. You should see the homepage with an upload option.

2. **Upload your manuscript**: click the upload button and pick your paper as a PDF. Important: it must be a real, text-searchable PDF (exported from Word or LaTeX), not a scan of printed pages, the AI needs to read the actual text. You should see a confirmation that it uploaded.

3. **Wait for the report**: the system runs your paper through its specialized AI reviewers. The author says this takes about 1 to 2 working days, and you usually get an email when it is ready.

4. **Download and apply the feedback**: open the PDF report (from the site or the email link) and work through its suggestions on methodology, rigor, and writing, improving your paper before sending it to co-authors or a journal.

## Notes & variations

- **Free-tier alternative**: the cloud version is free during testing. If you can code, a self-hosted version is on GitHub (github.com/robertjakob/rigorous); it gives full control but uses your own OpenAI API key, which costs money per run.
- **Common pitfall**: uploading a scanned image PDF. The reviewers cannot read it. Always use a text-based PDF from a document editor.
- **Tip for better results**: treat the AI feedback as a strong first pass, not the final word. Apply your own expertise, and if the tool lets you set custom criteria or a target journal, use that to tailor the review.
