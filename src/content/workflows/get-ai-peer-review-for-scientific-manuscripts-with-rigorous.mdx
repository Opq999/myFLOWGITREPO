## What you'll get
You'll receive a comprehensive PDF report with detailed feedback and actionable recommendations for your scientific manuscript. This system uses multiple specialized AI agents to analyze various aspects of your paper, from methodology and scientific rigor to writing quality, helping you improve it rapidly before submission.

## Tools you need
*   **Rigorous** (free): An AI-powered platform for scientific manuscript peer review.

## Steps
1.  **Access the Rigorous platform**: Open your mobile browser or computer and navigate to `https://www.rigorous.company`. You should see the Rigorous homepage, likely with an option to upload your manuscript.

2.  **Upload your scientific manuscript**: Look for a prominent button or section labeled "Upload your manuscript" or similar. Click it and select the PDF file of your scientific paper from your device. Ensure your manuscript is in a clear, text-searchable PDF format, not just scanned images. After selecting, you should see a confirmation message indicating your file has been successfully uploaded.

3.  **Await your comprehensive review report**: Once uploaded, the system will begin processing your manuscript using its 24 specialized AI agents. The author states that the review process typically takes "1-2 working days." You will likely receive an email notification when your detailed PDF report is ready for download.

4.  **Download and apply feedback**: When notified, return to the Rigorous platform or follow the link in your email to download your comprehensive PDF report. This report will contain detailed feedback and actionable recommendations across various aspects like methodology, scientific rigor, and writing quality. Carefully review the suggestions and use them to refine your manuscript before submitting it to co-authors or journals.

## Original source
This workflow is based on a project called 'AI Peer Reviewer' by rjakob, shared on Hackernews. The author developed this system to provide rapid, AI-powered feedback on scientific manuscripts, addressing the common delays in traditional peer review processes.

## Notes & variations
*   **Free-tier alternatives:** The cloud version of Rigorous is currently free during its testing phase, making it an excellent no-cost option for researchers. For those with programming skills and access to an OpenAI API key, a self-hosted version is available on GitHub (github.com/robertjakob/rigorous). This version offers full control and customization of the review process but will incur costs for OpenAI API usage.
*   **Common mistake or pitfall:** A common issue is uploading manuscripts as scanned images rather than text-searchable PDFs. The AI agents rely on being able to read the text directly, so ensure your PDF is generated from a document editor (like Word or LaTeX) for optimal analysis.
*   **Tip for better results:** While the system is powerful, always remember that AI feedback is a tool to assist, not replace, human judgment. Use the detailed recommendations to critically evaluate and improve your manuscript, but apply your own expertise and understanding of your field. The author mentions support for custom review criteria and target journals; if available in the cloud version, leverage these features to tailor the feedback to your specific needs.
