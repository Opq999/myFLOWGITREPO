## What you'll get
You will learn how to use AI tools to actively test your understanding and organize your study materials, leading to improved test scores. This approach works by making you engage with the material more deeply, rather than passively receiving answers, which helps solidify your learning.

## Tools you need
*   **ChatGPT** (freemium): A conversational AI for getting corrections and identifying mistakes in your understanding of a topic.
*   **NotebookLM** (freemium): An AI-powered notebook from Google that helps you organize information and create study aids like flashcards from your own documents.

## Steps

1.  **Test your understanding with ChatGPT**: Explain a concept in your own words to ChatGPT, then ask it to correct any mistakes.

    The author doesn't share their exact prompt; a starting point:

    ```
    I am studying [topic name]. I will explain a concept from this topic. Please act as my study partner: after I explain it, tell me if my explanation is accurate, point out any mistakes, and highlight areas where my understanding might be incomplete or unclear.
    ```

    After ChatGPT confirms it understands, type your explanation of the concept. You should get detailed feedback on your understanding, helping you identify gaps.

2.  **Create flashcards with NotebookLM**: First, read your study materials, then use NotebookLM to turn them into flashcards.

    First, thoroughly read and understand the notes or textbook chapters you want to study. Then, open NotebookLM in your web browser.

    Upload your study materials (e.g., PDF of a textbook chapter, your typed notes) to NotebookLM. Once uploaded, you can ask the AI to create flashcards.

    The author doesn't share their exact prompt; a starting point:

    ```
    Based on the uploaded documents, create a set of flashcards covering the key concepts and definitions from [specific chapter or section, e.g., 'Chapter 3: Newtonian Physics'].
    ```

    You should see NotebookLM generate a list of flashcards that you can use for self-testing and review.

## Original source
This workflow is inspired by the experience shared by Reddit user /u/ardelyo. They posted about how they initially struggled using AI for studying but found success by changing their approach to focus on active learning and using AI for correction and organization.

## Notes & variations
*   **Alternatives**: For step 1, you can use other freemium chat AIs like Google Gemini (gemini.google.com) or Claude (claude.ai) instead of ChatGPT. For step 2, other note-taking apps with AI features might offer similar flashcard generation capabilities.
*   **Common mistake**: The original author's mistake was relying on AI to give answers too quickly, which created a false sense of understanding. Avoid passively consuming AI-generated explanations without actively engaging your brain.
*   **Tip for better results**: Always read and try to understand your study material *before* using AI to summarize, explain, or create study aids. Use AI as a tool to enhance your learning process, not to replace it.
