## What you'll get
You will convert an image of handwritten math formulas or academic notes into clean LaTeX code or plain text. This workflow is useful for students and researchers who need to quickly digitize complex mathematical content without manual retyping, saving time and reducing errors.

## Tools you need
*   **Math2Tex** (free): A web application designed to convert images of academic content, especially math, into LaTeX or plain text.
*   **GPT** (freemium): A large language model that can be used as an alternative or for refining the output.
*   **Claude** (freemium): Another large language model that can be used for similar purposes as GPT.

## Steps
1.  **Open Math2Tex**: Navigate to the Math2Tex website in your web browser. You can access it at https://math2tex.com.
    You should see a single-page web application with an upload area and a preview pane.
2.  **Upload your image**: Click the upload button or drag and drop your image file (e.g., a photo of your notebook, a PDF screenshot) into the designated area.
    The tool will process the image, and you should see a preview of the detected content.
3.  **View the conversion**: Math2Tex will automatically convert the academic content from your image into LaTeX code and/or plain text. This appears in a real-time preview pane.
    You should see the converted text and/or LaTeX code displayed.
4.  **Copy the result**: Use the one-click copy button provided next to the converted text or LaTeX code.
    The converted content will be copied to your device's clipboard, ready to be pasted elsewhere.
5.  **Optional: Refine with an AI model**: If the output needs further refinement or if you want to convert the text to a different format, you can paste the copied content into a tool like GPT or Claude. The author doesn't share their exact prompt for this; a starting point:

    ```
    Please review and correct the following LaTeX code, ensuring mathematical accuracy and proper formatting:
    [Paste the copied LaTeX code here]
    ```

    You should receive a refined or alternative version of your text.

## Notes & variations
*   **Free Tier Viability**: Math2Tex is entirely free and web-based, making it highly accessible. GPT and Claude also have free tiers, which are generally sufficient for occasional use or refining outputs.
*   **Common Pitfall**: Ensure your uploaded image is clear, well-lit, and the handwriting is legible. Blurry or complex backgrounds can lead to conversion errors.
*   **Tip for Better Results**: For very complex or multi-line equations, try to take a photo that is as straight-on and close-up as possible. If using AI models for refinement, be specific in your prompts about the type of corrections or formatting you need.
