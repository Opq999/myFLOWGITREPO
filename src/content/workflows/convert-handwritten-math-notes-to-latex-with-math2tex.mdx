## What you'll get

A fast way to turn a photo of handwritten maths into clean, typed equations, no retyping by hand. Math2Tex reads your image and gives you the formula as LaTeX (the standard text format for writing maths in academic documents, where, for example, `x^2` means x squared) or as plain text you can paste anywhere. It saves students and researchers the slow, error-prone job of copying complex equations by hand.

## Tools you need

- **Math2Tex** (free): a website that converts a photo of maths or notes into LaTeX or plain text. Nothing to install.
- **GPT or Claude** (freemium): optional, for tidying up or reformatting the result. Free tiers are plenty for occasional use.

## Steps

1. **Open Math2Tex**: go to math2tex.com in your browser. You should see a single-page app with an upload area and a preview pane.

2. **Upload your image**: click upload, or drag in a photo of your notebook (or a screenshot of a PDF). The tool starts reading it, and you should see a preview of what it detected.

3. **Read the conversion**: Math2Tex turns the maths into LaTeX code and/or plain text in the preview pane, automatically. You should see the converted result appear.

4. **Copy the result**: use the one-click copy button next to the output. It goes to your clipboard, ready to paste into your document.

5. **Optional, tidy it up with AI**: if the output needs a fix or a different format, paste it into GPT or Claude:

   ```
   Review and correct this LaTeX, making sure the maths is accurate and the formatting is clean:
   [paste the copied LaTeX here]
   ```

   You should get a corrected or reformatted version back.

## Notes & variations

- **Free-tier viability**: Math2Tex is entirely free and runs in the browser. GPT and Claude's free tiers cover the occasional clean-up.
- **Common pitfall**: a blurry or shadowed photo. Clear, well-lit images with legible handwriting convert far more accurately.
- **Tip for better results**: for long or multi-line equations, photograph them straight-on and close-up. If you refine with AI, say exactly what you want fixed or reformatted.
