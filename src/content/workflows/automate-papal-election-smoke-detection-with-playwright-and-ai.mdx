## What you'll get

You will have a script that automatically checks for smoke signals from the Sistine Chapel chimney during a papal election. This workflow uses AI to verify if the smoke is white, indicating a successful election, and alerts you accordingly. It's a novel application of web automation and AI for a specific, real-world event.

## Tools you need

*   **Playwright** (freemium) - A tool for reliable end-to-end testing of modern web applications. Used here to interact with a hypothetical or real-time source of election information.
*   **Google Gemini Flash 2.0** (freemium) - A fast and efficient large language model from Google, used here to analyze images or text descriptions of the smoke.
*   **GitHub Actions** (freemium) - A CI/CD platform to automate workflows, used here to schedule and run the smoke detection script.

## Notes & variations

*   **Free Tier Viability**: GitHub Actions offers a free tier with a generous amount of minutes per month, making this workflow accessible without cost for personal use. Google Gemini Flash also has a free tier for its API.
*   **Common Pitfall**: Ensure your API keys are kept secure and are not hardcoded directly into your script. Use environment variables, especially within CI/CD platforms like GitHub Actions.
*   **Tip for Better Results**: If the AI struggles to accurately identify smoke color from images, consider if there's a text-based status update available from a reliable source that the script could parse instead, or provide the AI with more context about what 'white smoke' signifies in this specific event.
