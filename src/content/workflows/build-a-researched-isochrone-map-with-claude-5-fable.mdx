## What you'll get
You will get a fully researched and beautiful isochrone map that allows you to select various cities and visualize real travel time lines based on real-world data. This approach is powerful because it leverages advanced AI to research thousands of potential trip distances and make complex judgment calls that previous models struggled with.

## Tools you need
*   **Claude** (freemium): The AI platform hosting the advanced Fable model and the Claude Code environment for execution.

## Steps

1.  **Access Claude Code with Fable**: Open your web browser and navigate to the Claude AI platform. The author had early access to Claude 5 Fable, a Mythos-class AI model. If available, select Claude 5 Fable within the Claude Code environment. Otherwise, use the most advanced Claude model available to you, understanding that results may vary.

    You should see the Claude Code interface ready for your input.

2.  **Define your isochrone map project**: Paste the following detailed prompt into the Claude Code interface to instruct the AI on building your map. An isochrone map shows areas reachable within a specific travel time.

    ```
    i want you to build a fully researched and beautiful isochronic map that lets me pick various cities and see real isochronic lines based on real data. I want the design to be unique. You should take into account airports (and travel time to and from airports) trains, walking, driving. The data does not need to be live but should be real based on your research and data. You can start with a few cities but more general is better, this should be an entirely new project.
    ```

    The AI will begin processing your request and may ask clarifying questions or suggest an approach.

3.  **Agree to the design style**: The author's AI suggested creating the map in the style of the original 1881 isochrone map. If your AI makes a similar suggestion, agree to it to proceed.

    You should see the AI acknowledge your agreement and begin its multi-hour building session.

4.  **Iterate and refine the map**: The author used a couple of additional prompts with minor encouragement or feedback to improve the output. As the AI works, review its progress and provide prompts like "make it better" or specific feedback on elements you'd like adjusted or enhanced.

    ```
    Make it better by refining the visual clarity of the travel lines.
    ```

    You should see the AI incorporate your feedback, continuously improving the map's research and design.

5.  **Review the final isochrone map**: After the AI completes its extensive work, carefully examine the generated isochrone map. The author found the output to be impressive, capable of producing sophisticated results.

    You should have a fully researched and beautifully designed isochrone map, reflecting real travel data and unique design choices.

## Notes & variations
*   **Free-tier alternatives**: Claude 5 Fable is an advanced model that may not be available on free tiers. If you don't have access, try using the most capable free or freemium Claude model (e.g., Claude 3 Sonnet or Opus if available) or other advanced LLMs like Google Gemini Advanced or GPT-4. Be aware that these alternatives might not achieve the same level of sophistication or research depth as Fable.
*   **Common mistake**: Expecting a perfect, fully formed map from a single prompt. This workflow, especially for complex tasks, requires iteration and feedback. The author explicitly mentioned using "a couple of additional prompts" to refine the output.
*   **Tip for better results**: Be as specific as possible in your initial prompt, as the author was, detailing all requirements (cities, travel modes, data type, design goals). Then, be prepared to engage in a dialogue with the AI, providing clear, actionable feedback to guide its development process.
