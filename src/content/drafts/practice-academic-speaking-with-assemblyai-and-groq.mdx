## What you'll get

This workflow guides you to build a real-time AI voice coach that listens as you speak, transcribes your words, and provides instant feedback on clarity, tone, and structure. It's ideal for practicing academic explanations, thesis defenses, or interview responses, helping you articulate concepts more effectively.

## Tools you need

*   **AssemblyAI** (freemium) — For real-time speech-to-text transcription.
*   **Groq** (free) — To power the AI educator that analyzes your speech and provides feedback.

## Steps

1.  **Set up AssemblyAI for real-time transcription** — The author uses AssemblyAI's Streaming API to capture and transcribe audio live from a browser. You would typically integrate this by setting up a WebSocket connection to their API endpoint. The exact code implementation details are not provided in the excerpt, but the goal is to receive transcribed text chunks as you speak.
    *   **What to see:** As you speak into your microphone, you should see the text appearing on your screen almost instantaneously.

2.  **Integrate Groq for AI analysis** — The transcribed text from AssemblyAI is then sent to Groq. The author describes Groq acting as an "AI Educator Mode" that evaluates your speech as if you were explaining a concept to a domain expert. The specific prompts and API calls to Groq are not detailed, but the intention is to get feedback on metrics like fluency, coherence, redundancy, technical depth, and confidence.
    *   **What to see:** After speaking, you should receive a report card or feedback from the AI, grading your communication on various metrics.

3.  **Implement real-time feedback display** — The feedback generated by Groq needs to be displayed to the user in real-time. The author mentions an "Instant Report Card" delivered in real-time, visualizing your growth. This involves front-end development to show scores and constructive comments as they are received.
    *   **What to see:** A dashboard or section of your interface updates with scores (e.g., out of 10) and specific suggestions for improvement.

4.  **Enable practice and refinement** — The system is designed for iterative practice. If you stumble or want to rephrase, you can speak again, and the AI will provide updated feedback. The author emphasizes a "Retry Until It's Right" feature.
    *   **What to see:** The ability to speak multiple times and receive new feedback each time, allowing for continuous improvement.

5.  **Configure AI educator persona** — The AI is trained to respond like a "domain-specific educator." This means the prompts sent to Groq should instruct it to adopt this persona and evaluate the user's explanation from an expert's perspective.
    *   **What to see:** Feedback that is knowledgeable and critical, as if given by a professor or subject matter expert.

## Notes & variations

*   **Free-tier alternatives:** While AssemblyAI and Groq are mentioned, for simpler, non-real-time feedback, you could explore using free tiers of services like Google Cloud Speech-to-Text or OpenAI's Whisper API for transcription, and then use a free tier of a large language model like Gemini or Claude for analysis.
*   **Common pitfall:** Ensuring your microphone is properly configured and has clear audio input is crucial. Poor audio quality will significantly degrade the accuracy of both transcription and AI analysis.
*   **Tip for better results:** When practicing, try to speak at a natural pace and avoid excessive pauses or filler words. The AI is designed to help you identify and reduce these, so practicing as you normally would, but with awareness, will yield the most beneficial feedback.
