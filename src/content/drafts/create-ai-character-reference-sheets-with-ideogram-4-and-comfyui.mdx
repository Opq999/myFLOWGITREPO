## What this is, in plain English
This workflow describes how to create "character reference sheets" using AI tools like Ideogram 4 and ComfyUI. A character reference sheet is a collection of drawings of a character from different angles (front, side, back), with various expressions, poses, or outfits. Artists and designers use these sheets as a consistent visual guide when creating stories, games, or animations.

Ideogram 4 refers to the capabilities of Ideogram.ai, an AI tool that generates images from text descriptions. ComfyUI is a powerful, visual interface for advanced AI image generation, often used with Stable Diffusion models. It lets you build complex workflows by connecting different steps like building blocks.

Combining these tools helps you design characters and ensure they look consistent across many images. However, this is an advanced workflow because ComfyUI requires a good understanding of AI image generation concepts and often needs a powerful computer or cloud service to run.

## Notes & variations
*   **Do you even need this?**: For simple character concepts or single images, a direct text-to-image tool like Ideogram.ai or Midjourney might be sufficient. ComfyUI is best suited for users who need advanced control, specific consistency features, or want to build custom workflows that go beyond what simpler tools offer.
*   **Free-tier limits**: Ideogram.ai offers a free tier with daily image generation limits. ComfyUI is free software, but running it on your own computer requires a powerful graphics card (GPU). Alternatively, you can use cloud GPU services, which typically incur costs based on usage.
*   **Common pitfall**: Maintaining perfect consistency across multiple AI-generated images of the same character is very challenging. Small details can change, and getting exact matches for faces, clothing patterns, or body proportions often requires careful prompting, specific models, and advanced techniques like ControlNet or IP-Adapter.
*   **Tip for better results**: When using ComfyUI, explore community-made workflows for character consistency. Many users share their node setups (the visual blueprint of their workflow) that are specifically designed to keep characters consistent across different poses or views. Look for workflows that use ControlNet with pose estimation or IP-Adapter for style transfer.
