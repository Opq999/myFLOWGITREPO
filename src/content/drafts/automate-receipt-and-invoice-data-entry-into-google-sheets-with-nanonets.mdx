## What you'll get
You will set up an automated system that reads key information (like vendor, date, total, tax) from your receipts and PDF invoices. This data will then be automatically added to a Google Sheet, eliminating the need for manual typing. This approach works by using an AI-powered document parser to "read" your documents and extract specific fields, making it efficient for recurring invoices from common vendors.

## Tools you need
*   **Gmail** (freemium): For receiving vendor emails and setting up forwarding rules.
*   **Nanonets** (freemium): An AI-powered tool that extracts data from documents using templates you create.
*   **Google Sheets** (free): For storing the extracted data in an organized spreadsheet.

## Steps

1.  **Set up a dedicated email for receipts**: Create a new Gmail address (e.g., `receipts@yourbusiness.com`) or set up a filter in your existing Gmail account. This email will be where all your vendor invoices and receipts are sent or forwarded.
    *   *What to do*: Go to Gmail, create a new account, or go to Settings > See all settings > Filters and Blocked Addresses to create a new filter.
    *   *What you should see*: A new email address or a filter that can identify vendor emails.

2.  **Sign up for Nanonets and create a custom model**: Nanonets will be your document parser. You'll create a "model" (an AI template) specifically for invoices or receipts.
    *   *What to do*: Go to the Nanonets website, sign up for a free account. Choose to create a "Custom Model" and select "Invoice" or "Receipt" as the document type.
    *   *What you should see*: Your Nanonets dashboard with a new custom model ready for training.

3.  **Train your model with example documents**: Upload 2-3 examples of invoices or receipts from your most common vendors. You will then "annotate" these documents, showing the AI where to find specific pieces of information.
    *   *What to do*: In your Nanonets model, upload a PDF or image of a receipt/invoice. Use the Nanonets interface to draw boxes around fields like "Vendor Name", "Date", "Subtotal", "Tax", "Total", and "Currency". Label each box correctly. Repeat for 2-3 different documents, especially from different vendors if their layouts vary.
    *   *What you should see*: Nanonets will show the fields you've mapped. After annotating a few documents, the model will start to learn and predict these fields on new documents.

4.  **Connect Nanonets to your dedicated email**: Configure Nanonets to receive documents directly from your `receipts@` email address.
    *   *What to do*: In Nanonets, go to the "Integrations" or "Input" section for your model. Look for an option to connect via email. Nanonets will provide a unique email address (e.g., `yourmodel@nanonets.com`).
    *   *What to do (Gmail)*: In your `receipts@` Gmail account, set up email forwarding to the unique Nanonets email address. Alternatively, if you're using a filter, set it to forward matching emails to the Nanonets address.
    *   *What you should see*: Nanonets confirming that email input is configured.

5.  **Connect Nanonets to Google Sheets**: Set up an integration so that extracted data is automatically sent to a Google Sheet.
    *   *What to do*: In Nanonets, go to the "Integrations" or "Output" section. Select "Google Sheets". You will be prompted to connect your Google account and choose an existing Google Sheet or create a new one. Map the fields you extracted (Vendor, Date, Total, etc.) to specific columns in your Google Sheet. The author suggests adding a unique key like `file_id` or a hash; Nanonets often provides a document ID that can serve this purpose.
    *   *What you should see*: Nanonets confirming the Google Sheets integration is active, and your Google Sheet showing new column headers matching your mapped fields.

6.  **Test the full workflow**: Send a test invoice or receipt to your `receipts@` Gmail address.
    *   *What to do*: Send an email with a PDF or image attachment of an invoice/receipt to your `receipts@` email. Wait a few minutes.
    *   *What you should see*: The email should be forwarded to Nanonets, processed by your model, and the extracted data should appear as a new row in your connected Google Sheet. Check if all fields are correctly extracted.

## Notes & variations
*   **Free tier limits**: Nanonets offers a free tier that processes up to 100 pages per month. This is a good starting point for small businesses or freelancers with light data entry needs. If you process more, you'll need to upgrade to a paid plan.
*   **Common mistake**: Not training enough templates or training with low-quality documents. The AI model learns from your examples, so provide clear, representative invoices from your most frequent vendors. If a new vendor has a very different layout, you might need to create a new template or add an example from them.
*   **Tip for better results**: Start by creating templates for your 2-3 most common vendors first. Once those are working reliably, you can expand to more vendors or create a general "receipt" template for less frequent ones. Always review the first few extractions to ensure accuracy.
